An electrical engineer by education, I made the switch to IT-related work about 5 yrs into my career. I've done a bit of everything in the corporate IT space, including lots of coding in Visual Studio, lots of DB design and development with SQL platforms, hiring and leading developers, architecture and design, consulting, training, etc.

But, and this is a big but, probably 99% of my hands-on experience over the years has been with fat-client or embedded systems rather than web apps--particularly MVC. I've designed MVC apps and built DB infrastructure under them, but I've always had web developers doing the UI. I've made minor additions and changes, but I want to understand this tech stack at a deeper level. And because I recently left the corporate world, I finally have the time to do that.
